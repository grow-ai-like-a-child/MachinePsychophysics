Abstract

Cognitive control refers to the ability to flexibly coordinate thought and action in pursuit of internal goals. A standard probe is the family of conflict tasks, comparing performance on congruent vs. incongruent trials.

We perform the first large-scale psychophysics evaluation of this faculty in Vision‚ÄìLanguage Models (VLMs), testing 108 models on three classic paradigms and their more demanding ‚ÄúSquared‚Äù variants, yielding 2 220 trials per model.

VLMs exhibit human-like congruency patterns, but performance collapses when conflicts are hierarchical. Improvements follow a monotonic parameter‚Äìperformance trend, echoing resource-limited curves in human forced-response studies.

The benchmark shows that executive-function signatures can emerge from general-purpose learning at scale, while highlighting gaps that remain for robust cognitive control.

Emergent Congruency
